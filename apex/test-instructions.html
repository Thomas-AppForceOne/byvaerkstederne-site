Test instructions

Walkthrough for super users testing test.hackersbychoice.dk. This is a draft — please add anything you trip over.

What you are testing

The test tier mirrors what real visitors will eventually see, but with dummy data. Code here has cleared dev review; your job is to look for bugs, broken flows, awkward copy, and anything that would embarrass us in front of a real visitor.

Where to start

  1. Open test.hackersbychoice.dk in a clean browser tab (or incognito).
  2. Browse the site like a curious newcomer would: front page first, then click around. No specific path required.
  3. Try any forms you find (membership signup, feature suggestions, contact, etc.). Use obviously fake data — emails like test+yourname@example.com are ideal.
  4. Try the site on your phone too. Layout changes on small screens are an easy place to find bugs.

What to look for

  • Pages that don't load, or load with missing images / broken styling.
  • Buttons or links that go nowhere, or go to the wrong place.
  • Confusing copy — anything that made you stop and reread.
  • Forms that accept obviously bad input, or reject obviously good input.
  • Anything that feels slow, janky, or weird in a way you can't quite name.

How to report

For now: write down what you saw and the URL where you saw it, and send it to whoever pointed you at this site. Screenshots help. A "report a bug" form may exist in the footer — feel free to use that too if it works.

Things to know

  • The data here is fake. Don't expect real members, real events, or real personal information.
  • This site may break or change while you're testing. That's normal — we redeploy frequently.
  • If something is missing entirely (whole sections of the site invisible), that's intentional: features we haven't enabled here yet are hidden, not broken.
