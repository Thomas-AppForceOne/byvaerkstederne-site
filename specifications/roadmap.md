# Website Feedback & Roadmap

## Overview

Byværkstederne is a community built on participation and shared ownership. That same spirit should extend to the website itself. These three features create a feedback loop where members can flag problems, propose improvements, and see that their input actually shapes what gets built — turning the website from something done *for* the community into something built *with* it.

---

## Feature 1: Rapporter fejl (Bug Report)

Members encounter issues while using the site — a broken form, a page that won't load on mobile, a confusing flow. Right now there's no way to flag it without hunting down an email address or simply giving up. A persistent bug report overlay, available on every page, lets members capture the problem in the moment — including where they are on the site and what they were trying to do. Reports land directly with the team, nothing falls through the cracks.

---

## Feature 2: Foreslå en feature (Feature Suggestion)

Members often have ideas for how the digital tools could serve the community better. Without a place to put those ideas, they get shared in passing at a workshop evening and promptly forgotten. A dedicated suggestion page gives every member a voice and ensures good ideas are captured in a form the team can actually act on. It also signals that the community has genuine influence over the direction of the platform.

---

## Feature 3: Roadmap & Afstemning (Roadmap & Voting)

Transparency builds trust. When members can see what's been reported, what's being worked on, and what's coming next — and can vote on what matters most to them — they feel invested in the outcome. The roadmap page surfaces both bugs and feature suggestions as a living board, with a simple voting mechanism that helps the team prioritise based on real community need rather than guesswork. It closes the loop: report something, see it move through the process, know your vote mattered.

---

## Security Review

The site handles member accounts, personal data, and form submissions — and the new features add further surfaces where members interact with the platform in meaningful ways. Before the new features go live, a full security review of the entire site should be carried out. This includes the member registration and login flows, all existing and new forms, the way user-provided content is stored and displayed, and how the platform is exposed to the internet through its hosting setup. The goal is not to make the platform paranoid, but to ensure the trust members place in it is warranted and that no obvious vulnerabilities have been introduced as the site has grown.
